Hi, I'm Alec

As a software engineer with a passion for policy, I believe that the technologies we surround ourselves with directly reflect the values we hold.

While completing my Master's degree in environmental policy, I saw that emergency management seemed to focus more on response than prevention. Though mitigation was emphasized and funded, it was nearly always done so in response to some kind of natural diaster. In contrast, the software and data science projects I took on focused on solving today's and tomorrow's problems efficiently. As I took on increasingly complex projects in software engineering and data science, I was excited to grow into a position of leadership, making decisions on overall architecture of applications and mentoring colleagues that are as passionate about software as I am.

These days, I write in Go, Python, Bash, SQL, JavaScript, HTML, and CSS. My goal is to learn a new language every year by implementing it in a complex project in a way that makes sense. My favorite way to implement and deploy the code I write is through container systems, such as Docker. I find concurrency patterns in Go to be the most interesting topic as of late, but also enjoy reading about and implementing big data solutions in the cloud.

Lately, I have been thinking about what role software could play in improving our public education system, holding governments and politicians accountable, making the stock market accessible to the public, instilling general confidence in society, and decreasing my mile time.
